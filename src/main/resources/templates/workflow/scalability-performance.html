Performance Parameters

Low Traffic

Minimal user base or workload, suitable for internal applications or early-stage projects

Medium Traffic

Moderate user base or workload, common for business applications and established services

High Traffic

Heavy user base or workload, for consumer-facing applications or high-throughput systems

Rarely

Infrequent spikes in traffic or workload, predictable usage patterns

Occasionally

Periodic spikes in traffic or workload, such as during business hours or events

Frequently

Regular spikes in traffic or workload, requiring dynamic resource allocation

Always

Continuous high demand with unpredictable patterns, requiring constant scalability

Relaxed Response Time

Response time is not critical, suitable for background processes or batch operations

Standard Response Time

Moderate response time requirements, typical for most business applications

Strict Response Time

Fast response time is critical, for real-time applications or user-facing services

Low Cost Sensitivity

Cost is not a primary concern, performance and reliability take precedence

Balanced Approach

Seeking balance between cost and performance, typical for most business applications

High Cost Sensitivity

Cost optimization is critical, seeking the most efficient resource utilization
